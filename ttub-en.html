4D SIMULATION CT SCAN SYSTEM

The 4D simulation CT scanning system is a major step forward in the field of disease diagnosis and treatment. Integrating modern features such as OncoQuant, Lung VCAR, and Hepatic VCAR, this system provides not only detailed and accurate images of tumors and lesions but also minimizes radiation dose to the patient. Prospective Respiratory Gating and Deviceless 4D help optimize image acquisition, while technologies such as AsiR and SmartmA minimize dose while maintaining image quality. This system not only provides accurate diagnosis, but also enhances patient safety and comfort, while providing a powerful tool for medical professionals in managing and treating pathologies.

See more information

SPECT/CT TWO RECEIVER 16 SLICES

Brain-neuro SPECT data visualization and analysis software provides powerful tools to evaluate and quantify related pathologies from brain SPECT and SPECT/CT data. In addition, the software also supports analysis of radiographic data for many other organs such as lungs, thyroid, digestion, kidneys and bones. The software also includes functions to view and process cardiac scintigraphy data, along with the ability to automatically quantify myocardial perfusion in 3D. In particular, the software also quantitatively analyzes the cardiac blood pool by calculating volume and ejection fraction, providing a comprehensive tool for diagnosing and monitoring cardiovascular health.

How can we help you?

Cervical cancer vaccination is most effective for women aged 9 - 26 years old, not yet sexually active. For women who are married or still within the vaccination age group, the vaccine is still effective for this group of people. Pregnant people are not allowed to get this vaccine, but if they want to get it for prevention, they can still get it after giving birth, but remember to get all 3 shots.

We must distinguish between genetics and inheritance. All cancers are caused by genetic disorders or genetic factors, but cancers passed from parents to children are hereditary (accounting for 10%). There are several types of hereditary cancers such as breast cancer, colon cancer, thyroid cancer, children's bile cancer... but don't be too afraid, because there are very rare diseases such as familial polyposis that causes bowel cancer, or colon cancer. Medullary cancer of the thyroid only accounts for a very small proportion. It is currently not possible to prevent hereditary cancer, but you can pay attention to regular health checks and know how to detect the disease when there are no symptoms or pay attention to alarming symptoms.

Previous studies have shown that soybeans and foods made from soy contain isoflavones - estrogen-like substances that can cause breast cancer to develop. But isoflavones also have anti-cancer effects and are beneficial for the heart. The most recent research (2011) shows that soybeans and foods made from soy are good for patients after breast cancer treatment, especially Asian patients like us. You can safely drink soy milk and eat tofu if you like.

Chemotherapy: Is a method of using chemicals to kill cancer cells. This is a systemic therapy, affecting the patient's entire body to prevent the spread of cancer cells. The drugs chosen by the doctor will depend on the type of cancer, the stage of the disease as well as the patient's overall health. The purpose of chemotherapy is to prevent the spread of cancer cells to other organs of the body.

Radiation therapy: Is radiation therapy that uses high-energy waves to destroy cancer cells. Heat radiation will be introduced into the body through special devices. In addition to affecting cancer cells, radiation can also affect healthy cells. However, healthy normal cells will be able to heal themselves, while cancer cells cannot heal themselves.

In fact, chemotherapy and radiation therapy are frequently used together to treat certain types of cancer. This combination approach, called concurrent treatment, is recommended by doctors for cancer patients whose tumor cannot be removed with surgery and whose tumor is at risk of spreading to other areas of the body. body.
